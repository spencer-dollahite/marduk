Marduk

An audio-first assistive platform for macOS — vim-style modal navigation, spoken everything, no screen required. Free and open source (MIT).

Download

  1. Download Marduk.dmg (signed & notarized, always the latest release)
  2. Open it and drag Marduk into Applications
  3. Open Marduk — it installs itself and talks you through the rest, including the one permission it needs

Or with Homebrew:

brew install --cask spencer-dollahite/marduk/marduk

Requires an Apple Silicon Mac on macOS 26 (Tahoe).

What it is

  • Turns any document, PDF, or web article into an audiobook — press R and navigate the text by vim keys as it reads
  • Vim-style modal keyboard control — NORMAL, INSERT, VISUAL, and a command line with a spoken palette
  • Reads what's under your cursor; pauses and resumes your music around speech automatically
  • Announces password prompts, permission dialogs, and sheets out loud — even when they open outside your zoomed-in view
  • Runs as a quiet background daemon — no windows, no UI to see
  • Built for low-vision and audio-first use, by a user, in the open
  • Private by design: no telemetry, no analytics, everything stays on your Mac
